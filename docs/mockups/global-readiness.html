Global readiness & go-to-market

Strategy reference for scaling Match Point beyond Indonesia — social sport OS positioning, four interactive journeys, market fit, mockup maturity, monetization, and infrastructure after the prototype phase.

Executive summary

Match Point can serve the world as the operating system for racket sports communities — the place where players and communities build their entire sporting life: identity, competition, community ops, and social connection on one connected graph. Five sports in the mockup today (padel, tennis, pickleball, badminton, table tennis). Phase 0 validates UX across four interactive journeys (67 flow steps + 91 gallery screens); architecture is region-agnostic. Indonesia-specific today: launch data, integrations, and copy — not the core model.

QuestionAnswer

Are our sports global? Yes — padel (EU/LATAM/MENA/US), tennis (worldwide), pickleball (US-led, spreading), badminton (Asia/SEA/global), table tennis (global).

Problem & market fit

The gap nobody owns today

  • Official systems (federations, ILTL-style leagues) — sanctioned competition only; recreational club play is invisible.
  • Booking apps (Playtomic, KUYY) — court utilization, not portable reputation or cross-club rivalry.
  • Rating platforms (UTR, DUPR, WPR) — skill numbers without community history, endorsements, or social graph.
  • Social groups (WhatsApp, Instagram) — discovery and trust, but no structured rank, brackets, or audit trail.
  • Generic fitness social (Strava) — sharing habit without sport-format logic or fair-play gates.

Match Point sits at the intersection: structured sport identity + community operations + social stickiness. Players get a reason to open daily (pulse, feed, friends); clubs get ops tooling; the platform gets a measurable graph.

Who feels the pain (global ICP)

Market fit is strongest where padel/pickleball communities are dense, WhatsApp-organized, and underserved by federation data — Indonesia, UAE, Spain, and US metro areas are prime wedges.

Four journeys, one graph

The product is organized as four purpose-built homes that reinforce one network graph: Guest → Discovery Pulse ("why should I join?") · Player → Daily Hook + Player Passport ("why open every day?") · Community Admin → Community HQ ("how do I run and grow?") · Platform Admin → Ecosystem Console ("is the graph healthy?"). Competitors ship modules (competition, booking, rating); we ship the connected graph.

North star — social sport identity

Not replacing every app on day one — becoming the profile, rank, and community home players share everywhere:

  • Identity — Player Passport: 5-sport MP Rating, confidence %, achievements, share card + QR, endorsements
  • Community — discover, join, Mabar ladder, Community HQ for admins
  • Competition — club events, global tournaments, BoC seasons, sparring (ILTL-class module)
  • Social — feed, stories, messages, friends — mocked in Phase 0; real-time/push in Phase 5
  • Trust — verified matches, disputes, bracket eligibility, social moderation queue
  • Discovery + booking — court booking teaser (KUYY-class module in Phase 4)

What we are not (v1 production)

  • Primary court-booking marketplace — integrate Playtomic / KUYY / local POS
  • Replacement for UTR/DUPR/WPR official ratings — align display; optional API sync
  • TikTok-style infinite video — sport moments, share cards, and highlights only
  • Generic lifestyle social network — every social feature must tie back to play, rank, or community

Ranking & MP Rating (current mockup)

One engine per sport in rank.js — three user-facing numbers, one pipeline after each verified match.

LayerNamePurposeProduction note
1 MP Rating Trusted Glicko skill — tournament brackets, seeding, anti-sandbagging Never imported from WPR/UTR/DUPR; computed from verified results
2 MP pts (Mabar / Global) Club ladder & cross-community prestige — starts at 0 Delta from Glicko expected outcome × event weight
3 Reference hint ≈ WPR / ≈ UTR / ≈ DUPR orientation label Display only — not a registration gate

MP Rating lifecycle

StateConditionUIBracket access
Unrated0 matchesBelum dinilai / UnratedOpen only (or play first match)

Stable5+ matches, reliability ≥ 60%MP Rating X.XX ✓Full bracket range by MP Rating

Cross-club fairness

MP Rating 4.0 at Club A is not promised equal to 4.0 at Club B until cross-community play calibrates both sides. Badge Lintas klub ✓ unlocks Intermediate+ for Global tournaments and BoC when diversity thresholds are met (3+ cross-community matches, 20%+ opponent diversity in mock rules).

Regional expansion playbook

Per new country/region, run this checklist before marketing spend:

  1. Anchor communities — 3–5 pilot clubs with active weekly play
  2. Data source — one official or semi-official import (league, booking app, federation)
  3. Payment rail — local currency + tax/VAT rules for club SaaS
  4. Verification — phone SMS provider OR email + OAuth for markets with low SMS delivery
  5. Copy & labels — translate UI; localize “Mabar”, date formats, km/mi
  6. Support channel — WhatsApp (SEA), email (EU/US), in-app chat at scale
  7. Launch event — BoC mini-season or sparring between 2 flagship clubs

Monetization strategy

Revenue should align with who gets operational value — clubs and platform operators — while keeping player entry low-friction for network effects.

Revenue streams (recommended mix)

StreamModelWho paysWhen
Community Pro SaaS subscription per club (tiered by member count) Club admin v1.0 — core B2B
Event fees % platform fee on paid registrations Organizer / player v1.0 — tournaments & leagues
BoC / season sponsorship Branded seasons, featured slots Sponsors, federations v1.5 — after 50+ communities
Featured discovery Promoted community listings Clubs v1.5
Platform enterprise White-label or multi-region operator license Federations, chains v2.0
Player premium (optional) Advanced stats, custom share card themes Power users v2.0 — never paywall core rank

Pricing principles

  • Never paywall basic rank visibility — kills viral share cards
  • Charge for admin workload reduction (events, brackets, live scoring, analytics)
  • Localize currency — IDR for Indonesia, USD/EUR for export markets
  • Free tier: 1 community, limited events/month — enough to prove value
