Global readiness & go-to-market

Strategy reference for scaling Match Point beyond Indonesia — market fit, competition, mockup maturity, monetization, and infrastructure after the prototype phase.

Executive summary

Match Point can serve the world as a product category: portable player reputation and rankings across informal padel, tennis, and pickleball communities. The mockup phase validates UX and rules; architecture is region-agnostic. What is Indonesia-specific today is launch data, integrations, and copy — not the core model.

QuestionAnswer
Is the problem global? Yes — most play is outside federations; reputation is fragmented across clubs and apps.
Are our sports global? Yes — padel (EU/LATAM/MENA/US), tennis (worldwide), pickleball (US-led, spreading).
Do we replace UTR / DUPR / WPR? No — we complement them with community portability, endorsements, BoC, and club operations.
Launch strategy Indonesia-first density, global product design, regional adapters per market.
One-stop ambition Reputation + community ops + cross-club competition first; booking and full social feed are phase-2+.

Problem & market fit

The gap federations and booking apps do not fill

  • Official systems (federations, ILTL-style leagues) capture sanctioned competition only — recreational and club play is invisible.
  • Booking apps (Playtomic, court resellers) optimize court utilization, not long-term player reputation across clubs.
  • Rating platforms (UTR, DUPR, WPR) measure skill but not community history, endorsements, or inter-club rivalry narratives.
  • Social groups (WhatsApp, Instagram) drive discovery but leave no structured, portable record.

Who feels the pain (global ICP)

Market fit is strongest where padel/pickleball communities are dense, WhatsApp-organized, and underserved by federation data — Indonesia, UAE, Spain, and US metro areas are prime wedges.

Positioning & vision

What we are

The cross-community reputation layer for padel, tennis, and pickleball — community-first operations with a portable Global rank, fair-play gates, and flagship inter-community products (Battle of Communities, Community Sparring).

What we are building toward (north star)

A one-stop home for your racket-sport social identity — not replacing every app on day one, but becoming the profile and rank players share everywhere:

  • Identity — verified profile, per-sport skill bands (WPR / UTR / DUPR display), endorsements, share card
  • Community — discover clubs, join events, Mabar ladder, admin tools
  • Competition — club events, global tournaments, BoC seasons, sparring
  • Trust — match submission, disputes, eligibility, anti-sandbagging
  • Social (phased) — activity feed, highlights, rivalries, achievements — lightweight at first, not a generic social network

What we are not (v1 production)

  • Primary court-booking marketplace (integrate with Playtomic / local POS instead)
  • Replacement for UTR/DUPR/WPR official ratings (align display; link or import where APIs allow)
  • TikTok-style content platform (share cards and match moments, not infinite scroll video)

Regional expansion playbook

Per new country/region, run this checklist before marketing spend:

  1. Anchor communities — 3–5 pilot clubs with active weekly play
  2. Data source — one official or semi-official import (league, booking app, federation)
  3. Payment rail — local currency + tax/VAT rules for club SaaS
  4. Verification — phone SMS provider OR email + OAuth for markets with low SMS delivery
  5. Copy & labels — translate UI; localize “Mabar”, date formats, km/mi
  6. Support channel — WhatsApp (SEA), email (EU/US), in-app chat at scale
  7. Launch event — BoC mini-season or sparring between 2 flagship clubs

Monetization strategy

Revenue should align with who gets operational value — clubs and platform operators — while keeping player entry low-friction for network effects.

Revenue streams (recommended mix)

StreamModelWho paysWhen
Community Pro SaaS subscription per club (tiered by member count) Club admin v1.0 — core B2B
Event fees % platform fee on paid registrations Organizer / player v1.0 — tournaments & leagues
BoC / season sponsorship Branded seasons, featured slots Sponsors, federations v1.5 — after 50+ communities
Featured discovery Promoted community listings Clubs v1.5
Platform enterprise White-label or multi-region operator license Federations, chains v2.0
Player premium (optional) Advanced stats, custom share card themes Power users v2.0 — never paywall core rank

Pricing principles

  • Never paywall basic rank visibility — kills viral share cards
  • Charge for admin workload reduction (events, brackets, live scoring, analytics)
  • Localize currency — IDR for Indonesia, USD/EUR for export markets
  • Free tier: 1 community, limited events/month — enough to prove value
