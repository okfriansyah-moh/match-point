About Match Point

Product documentation for the four-journey operating system — Guest, Player, Community Admin, and Platform Admin — covering your entire racket sports life, rules, and release notes.

Vision & goals

Match Point is the operating system for racket sports communities — padel, tennis, pickleball, badminton, and table tennis. Most play happens outside official federations — recreational leagues, open play, and club events leave no portable record. We fix that with verified identity, match results, and rankings that travel with the player: your Player Passport, one 5-sport identity across every community.

The product is built as four journeys, each with a purpose-built home: Guest (Discovery Pulse — "why should I join?"), Player (Daily Hook Home — "why open this every day?"), Community Admin (Community HQ — "how do I run and grow my community?"), and Platform Admin (Ecosystem Console — "is the ecosystem healthy and fair?").

GoalWhat it means for users
Portability Your rank, endorsements, and community history follow you across clubs and cities.
Trust Organizer-submitted results, phone verification, dispute flow, and transparent rating rules.
Community-first Clubs run day-to-day play via Community HQ; the platform handles cross-community seasons, graph health, and global tiers.
Fair play Skill brackets, anti-sandbagging gates, and public rules — no hidden rating logic.

Four journeys

Match Point is organized as four purpose-built homes on one connected graph. Every feature must answer the core question for its journey — or it does not ship.

JourneyActorCore questionPrimary home
Guest Unauthenticated browser Why should I join Match Point? Discovery Pulse — trending communities, open events, read-only social, passport teaser
Player Authenticated member Why would I open this every day? Daily Hook Home — personalized pulse, play, social, rank, communities
Community Club / organizer admin How do I run and grow my community? Community HQ — module grid for events, members, feed, sparring, booking teasers
Platform Match Point superadmin Is the ecosystem healthy and trustworthy? Ecosystem Operator Console — graph KPIs, triage, community pipeline, social moderation

Competitor categories map to modules of this OS — competition apps (e.g. ILTL) ≈ Competition module; discovery/booking apps (e.g. KUYY) ≈ Discovery + Booking module; all-in-one sport apps (e.g. AYO) ≈ Booking + Sparring module. The moat is the connected graph, not any single module. We follow a Reclub-style sequence: social sport + communities + rank first (Phases 0–3); court booking integrates in Phase 4. Your racket sports life

Where players and communities build their entire sporting life — one 5-sport identity, portable rank, social connection, and community operations under a single shell.

Player Passport

Unified identity rollup across padel, tennis, pickleball, badminton, and table tennis — MP Rating per sport, achievements trophy case, communities, friends, and a share card with QR for add-friend. Entry lives on Profile; guests see a blurred passport teaser on the conversion ladder.

Daily Hook Home

Authenticated home answers why open today with personalized pulse cards — upcoming play, rating delta, social highlights, booking teaser, and community activity. Guest home runs a parallel Discovery Pulse with public stats and soft gates (no hard login wall).

Social layer

  • Feed & stories — composer, story rings, 8-post demo feed; guests see public posts + blurred member-only stubs
  • Messages — inbox with demo threads (member-only; hidden for guests)
  • Friends — followers, suggested players, follow toggles; passport QR for add-friend
  • Achievements — 6 trophies on passport pride strip and share card
  • Soft gates — like, comment, RSVP, and compose prompt inline login; no full-screen login wall while browsing

Navigation & play

Signed-in bottom nav: Home · Play · Social · Rank · Communities. Guest bottom nav (4 tabs): Explore · Play · Social · Rank — same shell, reduced scope. Profile, messages, settings, and About live in the avatar dropdown (not bottom nav). Quick actions on home: Catat Hasil Match (~30s) and Book a court teaser — no floating action button in the current flow. Court booking is a Phase 4 roadmap module (AYO/KUYY-class); organize play via open mabar, player challenges, and events now. See COMPETITIVE-PLAN.md.

Guest conversion ladder

  1. See an alive platform — Discovery Pulse (523 communities, trending clubs, open events)
  2. Browse — community pages, events, read-only social, public leaderboards
  3. Hit a soft gate — RSVP, like/comment, personal rank → inline auth modal
  4. Claim identity — register → Player Passport → join community → Daily Hook unlocks

Communities

A community is a club or group (open or invite-only) tied to one primary sport. Players can belong to multiple communities with one home community on their profile.

  • Discovery feed — find clubs by sport, distance, and access type
  • Community page — cover card, member feed, join CTA (Match Point header chrome)
  • Platform approval for new communities and featured listings
  • Community HQ — KPI strip (members, active week, next event, pending requests) + bento grid: Events, Registrations, Live Session, Members, Feed, Sparring; Booking & Finance placeholders
  • Community types taxonomy — club, league, academy, social group (extensible in community-data.js)

Which tournament should I join?

  1. No verified match yet → Open only, or play your first match
  2. Provisional (1–4 matches) → Beginner or Open
  3. Stable → compare your MP Rating to the bracket range on the event card

Bracket Class = who can register. Global Tier 1–3 = how many Global pts the winner earns.

Global tournaments

Platform admin runs cross-community tournaments (Tier 1–3) that feed Global pts. Club tournaments use the same bracket classes with MP Rating gates.

Battle of Communities (BoC)

Flagship seasonal inter-community product — Match Point branded only (not third-party league co-branding). Operators with 100+ communities use in-app draw instead of external randomizers; squads and penalties are tracked in-platform.

AspectRule
Who runs itPlatform admin
ScaleMany communities (10–100+), group draw → round-robin → knockout
Rubric3 doubles + 1 singles war points; optional King/Queen (2 pts)
Scoring8 games per set (configurable)
SquadsDue 3 days before each fixture; late → −1 game per set
Rank weight100% of a standard Mabar fixture delta
AuditDraw seed, operator, timestamp logged immutably

Ratings & fair play

MP Rating (Glicko-2-lite) per sport: one trusted number from verified matches. Reference hints (≈ WPR, ≈ UTR) are optional — never the gate.

Is MP Rating fair across clubs?

If Club A is stronger than Club B and both only play internally, MP Rating 4.0 at Club A is not promised to equal 4.0 at Club B — yet.

ScopeBadgeFair for
Club-local Klub / Club Same-club brackets, Mabar leaderboard
Cross-community Lintas klub ✓ Global tournaments, BoC, inter-club sparring

When clubs meet, verified results calibrate both sides via Glicko expected-outcome — upsets move ratings fairly. Run inter-club sparring to earn Lintas klub ✓.

Anti-sandbagging

  • Registration: min/max MP Rating per bracket class
  • Play-up allowed when configured; play-down blocked by default
  • Global / inter-club Intermediate+ requires Lintas klub ✓ calibration
  • BoC squad slots re-check eligibility before lock

Sports & scale

The same ranking rules apply to all five sports — padel, tennis, pickleball, badminton, and table tennis.

  • One identity, separate MP Rating and ladder per sport — no cross-sport bleed
  • Same bracket vocabulary (Beginner / Intermediate / …) — only numeric scale differs
  • Adding a sport = new scale calibration in SPORT_PROFILES, not new rules

Platform operations

Platform admin operates the Ecosystem Operator Console — graph health first, triage second, growth modules alongside competition tooling.

  • Ecosystem overview — graph KPIs (10,842 players · 523 communities · 1,180 matches · 3,402 posts · 4,216 DAU · 0.6% dispute rate), network loops, sport mix
  • Triage card — top 3 SLA items elevated on overview (disputes, incomplete docs, fast-track approvals)
  • Triage inbox — communities, featured events, disputes (SLA-sorted review → approve/reject)
  • Community pipeline — onboarding funnel: pending, approved, rejected, growth stages
  • Social moderation — reported posts, spam, community feed flags
  • Matches queue — pending submissions across clubs
  • Analytics — lifecycle, rank distribution, activity feed
  • Battle of Communities wizard — season config, draw, fixture calendar
  • Feedback widget — mockup testers send notes via WhatsApp or email (not stored on demo site)
